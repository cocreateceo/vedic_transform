Step 1 

1. Wake Up Early (preferably 5 AM / or 20 mins before your normal time)
	•	Get out of bed calmly
	•	Hydrate with a glass of warm water
	•	Avoid phone use for the first 20–30 minutes

2. Center Your Breath (2–5 minutes)
	•	Sit upright
	•	Take slow, deep breaths
	•	Inhale for 4 seconds, exhale for 6 seconds
	•	Allow the body and mind to settle

3. Connect to Awareness / Source (1–2 minutes)
	•	Sit quietly in stillness
	•	Bring attention to the present moment
	•	Gently become aware of your inner state
	•	Feel connection to the universal energy (Brahman)

4. Gratitude & Thought Alignment (1–2 minutes)
	•	Mentally list 3 things you’re grateful for
	•	Set one positive intention for the day
	•	Replace any negative thought with a constructive one

5. Manifestation & Energy Alignment (2 minutes)
	•	Visualize your key goal for the next 48 days
	•	See yourself already succeeding
	•	Invite clarity, strength, and guidance (“energy download”)
	•	Hold that image for a few breaths\


Vedic Transformation – Step 2B: mind and energy intake 
⸻

Step 2B: Food for the Mind – Simple Steps

1. Start the Day with Positive Inputs (Before 8 AM)
	•	Read or listen to uplifting content: a scripture verse, or 5 minutes of positive thinking philosophy.
	•	Avoid news, social media, arguments, and negativity in the first hour.
	•	This sets your “mental state” for the whole day.

⸻

2. Consume positive  Mental Nutrition

Just like food for the body, the mind also digests what you expose it to.
Choose inputs that create clarity, peace, and inspiration:
	•	Mantras, spiritual texts, inspirational books
	•	Nature, silence, mindful music
	•	Conversations with kind, uplifting people

⸻

3. Avoid Tamasic Mental Intake (Especially After Sunset)

Just as we don’t eat heavy food late, avoid heavy mental food after evening:
	•	Violent content, gossip, toxic conversations
	•	Overthinking or doom-scrolling
	•	Complaints, drama, blame
These weaken the mind, exactly like junk food weakens the body.

⸻

4. Remove Negative People & Energies (Daily Practice)
	•	Reduce interaction with people who drain, criticize, complain, or manipulate.
	•	Limit exposure to people, phone, media, or groups that pull you down.
	•	Create healthy boundaries — protect your mental space.
	•	Your mind becomes the company you keep — “Satsang transforms; dussang destroys.”

⸻

5. Add Positive, High-Vibration Influences

Choose what (and who) you “feed” your mind:
	•	Supportive people who encourage growth
	•	Teachers/mentors who inspire you (identify a few who are willing to help)
	•	Environments that feel peaceful and energizing (consciously find and nurture them)
	•	Silence, prayer, meditation as “mental superfoods”
These nourish the mind and uplift energy naturally.

⸻

Optional Add-Ons (Powerful Vedic Mind Practices)
	•	1 minute - gratitude every morning.
	•	2 minutes - deep breathing before meals.
	•	Slow your speech — during key focus moments - it slows the mind.
