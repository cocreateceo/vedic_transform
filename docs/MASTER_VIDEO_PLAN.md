### V2 — Introduction to Sattvic Eating (18 min)

**Pillar:** Nutrition + Fasting · **Use:** Library card `content-vedic-nutrition` · **Voice:** Indian English male, 0.9x · **16:9**

```
Three thousand years before nutrition labels existed, Ayurveda classified every food into one of three categories — Sattvic, Rajasic, Tamasic. The category determines not just what your body does with the food, but what your mind does with the day. In the next eighteen minutes I will teach you the framework and give you one Sattvic recipe to start tomorrow.

The three gunas — Sattva, Rajas, Tamas — are the three fundamental qualities of all material existence. Sattva is clarity, lightness, intelligence. Rajas is activity, passion, restlessness. Tamas is dullness, heaviness, inertia. Every food carries these qualities and transmits them to your mind and body.

Sattvic foods are those that produce clarity. Fresh fruit eaten the same day it ripens. Soaked nuts and seeds. Whole grains like basmati rice and barley. Ghee — clarified butter, the most sacred Ayurvedic fat. Mung dal — the only legume Ayurveda considers fully Sattvic. Unhomogenized milk. Raw honey. Herbal teas. These foods leave you feeling light, alert, and centered.

Rajasic foods are stimulating. Onion. Garlic. Chili. Coffee. Chocolate. Refined sugar. Very spicy food. These are not bad. They are useful in small amounts when you need activation. But they create restlessness when overused.

Tamasic foods are dulling. Leftover food more than three hours old. Deep-fried food. Heavily processed food. Alcohol. Meat — in the classical Vedic view, all animal flesh is Tamasic because it carries the suffering of the animal. Tamasic foods leave you sluggish, foggy, and emotionally heavy.

The transition to Sattvic eating is gradual. Do not aim for 100 percent Sattva overnight. Aim for 60-30-10. Sixty percent Sattvic, thirty percent Rajasic, ten percent Tamasic. This is sustainable and aligned with modern life.

Beyond what you eat, Ayurveda has specific rules for how you eat. Eat your largest meal at noon, when your digestive fire — Agni — is strongest, aligned with the sun at its peak. Sit while eating. Do not drink large amounts of liquid 30 minutes before or after a meal. And eat to 75 percent fullness. Leave a quarter of your stomach empty for the digestive process.

Now let me show you the most foundational Sattvic recipe. Kichari. Three ingredients, complete nutrition, easy to digest.

Heat one tablespoon of ghee in a saucepan over medium heat. Add one teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them sizzle for fifteen seconds. Add one quarter teaspoon of turmeric, a one-inch piece of grated ginger, and a pinch of salt. Stir for ten seconds.

Add half a cup of basmati rice and half a cup of split yellow mung dal. Both have been soaked for thirty minutes and rinsed. Stir to coat with the ghee and spices. Add four cups of water. Bring to a boil. Reduce to low heat. Cover and simmer for 25 minutes, until rice and dal are soft and the consistency is like thick porridge.

Garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime. Serve warm. This single bowl provides complete protein, easy carbohydrates, healthy fat, and key spices for digestion. Eat it for lunch or dinner. Many practitioners do a three-day kichari cleanse twice a year.

In the first week of Sattvic eating, expect lighter sleep, sharper mornings, and a mild detox headache on day two or three. This is the body releasing old metabolic byproducts.

Namaste.

### V6 — Sankalpa: The Vedic Science of Manifestation (13 min)

**Pillar:** Divine Manifestation · **Use:** Library card `content-manifestation` · **Voice:** Indian English male, 0.9x · **16:9**

```
Most manifestation teaching is half-truth, and the other half is what goes wrong. The Vedic tradition has a precise term for this work. Sankalpa. The rules for using it are very different from what social media will tell you. In thirteen minutes I will teach you the three properties a real Sankalpa must have, the most common mistake, and how to plant one today.

The word Sankalpa is composed of two roots. Sam, meaning together or completeness. Kalpa, meaning intention or creative will. Together — the unified creative will. The classical definition is the mental resolve formed at the level of identity, not desire. Not "I want X." Rather, "I am the kind of person who X."

This is the first crucial distinction. Modern goal-setting frames the goal as a thing you produce. Vedic Sankalpa frames it as a person you become. The difference matters because the unconscious mind responds to identity statements, not to wish-list statements.

Three properties define a real Sankalpa.

Property one. Present tense. Never "I will be steady" or "I want to be steady." Always "I am steady." The mind cannot act on future tense. The mind acts only on what it considers already true. By stating the Sankalpa in present tense, you give your nervous system permission to operate as if the outcome is already so.

Property two. Affirmative. Never "I will not get angry." Never "I am not afraid." The unconscious deletes negations. If you say "I am not afraid," the unconscious hears "I am afraid." Use the positive form. "I am calm." "I am steady." "I am free."

Property three. Plausible to the believer. A Sankalpa your gut rejects has no traction. If you have been anxious for ten years and you write "I am the most peaceful person in the world," your nervous system will reject the statement. Build belief progressively. Start with something your gut accepts as 60 percent true today. "I am learning to be steady." "I am becoming present."

The most common mistake. Trying to manifest objects. Cars. Money. Houses. Vedic Sankalpa works at the level of identity, which then produces results in the material world. Reverse the order — start with objects — and the system fails. The Bhagavad Gita is explicit. You have a right to action, never to the fruits of action. Sankalpa is the action — at the level of being.

How to plant the Sankalpa. Write it in a small notebook. One line. Same line, every morning, for at least 48 days. Speak it aloud once after writing. Repeat it silently at the threshold of sleep, when the unconscious is most receptive to suggestion. Revisit it at dawn, the most powerful window for new pattern installation.

## Final notes

- **Stay consistent on tone:** every prompt above carries the brand voice
  (warm, didactic, anti-hype, Vedic). Don't let a tool's auto-script
  rewrite it into clickbait.
- **Captions on EVERY video:** 80% of mobile viewers watch with sound off.
  CapCut's auto-caption gets them done in 30 sec per video.
- **Music — same bed for the whole set:** Aakash Gandhi "Dhanam" or
  "Drone in D" (YouTube Audio Library, free). Repeating the music across
  videos creates auditory brand recognition.
- **Color grade everything:** apply CapCut's "Warm Film" or "Cinematic"
  LUT to all video output for visual consistency.
- **Test the first one fully** before batch-generating. Catch issues early.
